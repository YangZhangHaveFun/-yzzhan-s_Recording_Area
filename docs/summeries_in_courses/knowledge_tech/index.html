Probability

Joint probability : P(A,B) means the probability of both A and B occurring.

Conditional probability : P(A|B) means the probability of A occurring given the occurrance of B $ $

Multiplication rule: P(A ∩ B) = P(A|B)P(B) = P(B|A)P(A).

In the view of KT, there’s more information related to P(A) and P(A|B). - P(A|B), the posterior, is the degree of belief having accounted for B.

> the probability of A occurring, given no additional knowledge about A

  • P(A), the prior, is the initial degree of belief in A.

    the probability of A occurring, given background knowledge about event(s) B leading up to A

Distribution

  • Bionomial distribution : results from a series of indenpendent trials with only two outcomes. The probability of an event with probability p occurring exactly m out of n times is given by : $$ P(m,n,q) = (n m)p^m(1-p)^n P(m,n,q) = (p^m(1-p)^n)*n!/(n-m)!m! $$

  • multinomial distribution : n results from a series of independent trials with more than two outcomes. $$ P(X1= x1, X2 = x2,…, Xn = xn) = (sum{i}{xi})! multi{i}{p{i}^{x{i}}/x_{i}!} $$

Information Theory

Consider a message M composed of distinct symbols w1,…,wn, where each symbol wi has a frequency fi. The total length of the message is |M| = \sum{i}{f{i}}. > The Information Theory indicates the minimum length encoding of the message is to allcoate log2(fi/|M|)

Entropy -> A measure of unpredictablity/information content -> the theoretical minimum length of the message in the context of the provided information.

        -> Measures the average missing information on a random source, or the unevenness of a probability distribution.

$$ H(x) = -\sum{i=1}^{n}P(i)\log{2}P(i) $$

  • The range is determined by the possible number of outcomes.
  • 0 ≤ Entropy ≤ log(n), where n is number of outcomes.
  • Entropy=0 (minimum entropy) when one probability is 1, others 0.
  • Entropy=log(n) (maximum entropy): when all probabilities have equal values of 1/n.
